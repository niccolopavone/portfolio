Freelance

Designer

2008–2019 Palo Alto · Providence · New York · Auckland · Milan · Barcelona

I come from a Classical education in Humanities and the Arts, synthesizing the core of my studies in a thesis titled Code is Poetry. This work embraced the idea that code—in all its forms, whether visual, linguistic, musical, or rooted in human-machine interaction—is poetry: the highest, yet most accessible and fundamental form of language.

My journey has taken me around the world, evolving from small independent projects to larger-scale ventures, deeply influenced by my academic experiences abroad. Inspired by my travels, peers, and mentors, I studied at Stanford (Palo Alto), the Rhode Island School of Design, and Media Design School in Auckland, New Zealand. Ultimately, I returned to Italy, anchoring my career in my hometown of Milan and exploring opportunities within various digital firms.

There, I cultivated my passion for visual communication, initially through the web and emerging formats like animation and interactive storytelling—mediums I continue to regard as some of the most impactful in the field. I started as a freelance designer and developer on small to mid-sized projects before advancing to more structured roles as a graphic designer, project manager, and developer at Milan-based agencies such as Cayenne and later Accenture.

I have been trusted to create high-end digital experiences for visionary clients delivering bespoke websites, applications, and brand identity systems that embody the highest standards of creativity, functionality, and innovation.
